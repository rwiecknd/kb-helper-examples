Context: summary of account/data lifecycle 

The article is a high-level summary of what happens with your Google account and content when you leave Notre Dame. There are links within each section that provide more information.

Retirees typically retain access to their ND NetID@nd.edu, but visit this article for more information and your responsibility.

 

Students

The information below applies to students who graduate.

To help explain, we will use May 2024 graduates as an example. And note, that summer 2023 grads and December 2023 grads are considered May 2024 graduates since they can go through May commencement events.

  • May 2024 graduates lose access to log in to their NetID@nd.edu account ~60 days after the last class day for the spring semester
  • Some graduate programs (MBA, Law) get more than 60 days before they lose access to their NetID account
  • Reminder emails are sent to students about losing access to their NetID@nd.edu account. We send 7 or 8  emails starting 30 days before their nd.edu account is disabled
  • After the 60 days, the NetID@nd.edu account is no longer active
  • We suspend the Google account 14 days after the NetID@nd.edu account is inactivated

When the Google account is suspended

  • We rename the account/email address from NetID@nd.edu to delete__NetID@nd.edu (that's 2 underscores)
  • All collaborators will retain access to the content previously shared with them
  • The Gmail and Google Drive content is no longer associated with the NetID@nd.edu account
    • Gmail and Google Drive content is associated with the delete__NetID@nd.edu account
    • Delete__NetID@nd.edu is not a person but an account holding onto the Google Drive content for collaborators until the account is deleted in a year (see lifecycle policy link below)
  • Renaming the account allows any active collaborator (ex: current employee, current student, etc.) visual information that the account is in a pending deletion state
  • We do not change the first and last name of the account
  • We remove all aliases previously attached to the account such as the NetID@nd.edu and firstlastname@nd.edu
  • With all aliases removed, to find content emailed from the student or owned by the student in Google, it is best to search by the student’s name or delete__NetID@nd.edu (that's 2 underscores)
  • Email sent to a suspended Google account is not delivered (i.e. it bounces)
  • We will delete the delete__NetID@nd.edu Google account and all the data owned by the account (Gmail, Drive, etc.) a year after the NetID@nd.edu account is suspended.
  • If the graduated student returns to the university before the account is deleted, the person will get their old NetID again, but will have no access to any content that they owned in Google My Drive
    • And will not have access to their previous email
  • See the university policy on content deletion

Faculty, Staff, Affiliates 

  • Retirees, for information on what occurs with your NetID@nd.edu account and Google account, please see this article

The below applies to all non-student accounts such as Faculty, Staff, and Affiliates. We will call this group employees in the information below

  • For an employee leaving Notre Dame, we ask that you follow the information contained in this article.
    • Following the steps allows a smooth transition for your area when you leave ND
    • The article covers items such as creating an out-of-office email reply and who to contact once you leave, transferring content of Box, Drive, OneDrive, Qualtrics, etc, transferring calendar events that you own, and other important information
    • Also please review the information about the use of your NetID@nd.edu account that you may have created for personal services such as TV streaming, banks, etc., and what you need to do
  • An employee who leaves Notre Dame will have their NetID@nd.edu account suspended the day after their last work date
  • The Google account will be suspended 14 days after the NetID@nd.edu account is suspended 
    • Email will be delivered to the account during the 14 day period between the NetID account being suspended and before the Google account is suspended
    • Email sent to a suspended Google account is not delivered (i.e. the sent email bounces)
    • Email sent to a suspended Google account will be not forward to another (i.e. the sent email bounces)

  • Renaming the account allows any active collaborator (ex: current employee, current student, etc.) visual information that the account is in a pending deletion state

What occurs if the graduated student or previous employee returns to Notre Dame before their Google account is deleted?

  • The person will get their previous NetID@nd.edu account but will have no access to emails or Google content that they had access to when they left Notre Dame
