Students

The information below applies to students who graduate.

May 2024 graduates are used as an example. Summer 2023 and December 2023 graduates are considered May 2024 graduates because they can participate in May commencement events.

  • May 2024 graduates lose access to log in to their NetID@nd.edu account approximately 60 days after the last class day for the spring semester.
  • Some graduate programs (e.g., MBA, Law) receive more than 60 days before NetID account access is removed.
  • Reminder emails are sent, starting 30 days before account deactivation — approximately 7 or 8 messages in total.
  • After 60 days, the NetID@nd.edu account is no longer active.
  • The Google account is suspended 14 days after the NetID account becomes inactive.

When the Google account is suspended:

  • The email address is renamed from NetID@nd.edu to delete__NetID@nd.edu (that's two underscores).
  • Collaborators retain access to previously shared content.
  • Gmail and Google Drive content is disassociated from NetID@nd.edu and instead associated with delete__NetID@nd.edu.
    • delete__NetID@nd.edu is a holding account for content and not a person.
    • This temporary account is deleted one year after the original NetID was suspended.
  • This renaming alerts collaborators that the account is in a deletion state.
  • First and last name of the account remains unchanged.
  • All aliases (e.g., NetID@nd.edu, firstlastname@nd.edu) are removed.
  • Search for content using the person’s name or delete__NetID@nd.edu.
  • Email sent to a suspended account is undeliverable (bounces).
  • One year after suspension, the account and all data are permanently deleted.
  • If the student returns before deletion, they will regain their NetID but will have no access to Google My Drive content or previous email.
    • They will not have access to their previous email.
  • See the university policy on content deletion

Faculty, Staff, Affiliates

  • Retirees can learn more about what happens to their NetID@nd.edu and Google account by reading this article.

The following applies to all non-student accounts (e.g., Faculty, Staff, and Affiliates), referred to as employees throughout:

  • Employees leaving Notre Dame should follow instructions in this article to ensure a smooth transition for their department.
    • Details include: setting out-of-office replies, transferring Box, Google Drive, OneDrive, Qualtrics content, calendar transfers, and more.
    • Also includes advice on NetID@nd.edu use for personal services and necessary steps to take.
  • NetID@nd.edu account is suspended the day after the employee’s last work date.
  • Google account is suspended 14 days after NetID account suspension:
    • Email is delivered during the 14-day window before the Google suspension.
    • Once suspended, emails bounce and are not forwarded elsewhere.

When the Google account is suspended:

  • Email address is renamed from NetID@nd.edu to delete__NetID@nd.edu (two underscores).
  • Collaborators retain existing shared access.
  • All content is reassociated with delete__NetID@nd.edu.
    • This temporary holding account is not an individual.
    • Deletion occurs one year after NetID suspension.
  • Renaming signals account's pending deletion.
  • Account's name is not changed.
  • Aliases including NetID@nd.edu and firstlastname@nd.edu are removed.
  • Search by name or delete__NetID@nd.edu for associated content.
  • Permanent deletion occurs after one year.
  • If the person returns within that year:
    • They get back their NetID, but will not regain access to their My Drive content.
    • They will not regain access to any previous email.
  • See the university policy on content deletion

Returning to Notre Dame

  • If a graduated student or previous employee returns to Notre Dame before their Google account has been deleted, they will regain access to their previous NetID@nd.edu but will have no access to any Google content or email they had while previously at Notre Dame.
  • It’s important to understand: once a person leaves the university, access to former content is permanently forfeited, even upon return.
